Introduction

John 15:1–8 English Standard Version

15 “I am the true vine, and my Father is the vinedresser. 2 Every branch in me that does not bear fruit he takes away, and every branch that does bear fruit he prunes, that it may bear more fruit. 3 Already you are clean because of the word that I have spoken to you. 4 Abide in me, and I in you. As the branch cannot bear fruit by itself, unless it abides in the vine, neither can you, unless you abide in me. 5 I am the vine; you are the branches. Whoever abides in me and I in him, he it is that bears much fruit, for apart from me you can do nothing. 6 If anyone does not abide in me he is thrown away like a branch and withers; and the branches are gathered, thrown into the fire, and burned. 7 If you abide in me, and my words abide in you, ask whatever you wish, and it will be done for you. 8 By this my Father is glorified, that you bear much fruit and so prove to be my disciples.

Let’s review what we learned in our previous message on pruning:

·         Pruning is cutting away branches on a plant so that the plant can be fruitful and healthy. For example, when a gardener diligently prunes a rosebush, that bush will produce beautiful and full roses.

·         A gardener prunes three types of branches and buds. First are healthy buds or branches that are not the best ones. Second, there are sick branches that will not get well, and third, there are branches that need to be removed for the healthy ones to thrive.

·         Plants that bear flowers and fruits always produce more buds than the plant can sustain. These plants need a gardener to ensure they produce flowers and fruits.

·         God is the gardener, Jesus is the vine, and we are the branches. God will prune the Church to ensure we produce maximum spiritual fruit (John 15:8).

 

How the Father Prunes Us

John 15:7 English Standard Version

7 If you abide in me, and my words abide in you, ask whatever you wish, and it will be done for you.

In this message, we will explore how the Father prunes us. Our answer is in verse 7. The Father prunes us with the Word of God.

Remember, pruning involves cutting. As we abide in the Word of God, that is, read, study, and prayerfully meditate upon Scripture, we will experience cutting conviction and stinging correction (Prov. 6:23; Acts 2:37). That is normal! It is abnormal to read the Word of God and not be corrected!

Since the primary way that the Father prunes his people is through the Word of God, we must be diligent to abide in Scripture so we can be sensitive to what the Father wants to prune in our lives, such as:

Good activities we are not called to do;

Good ministries that have come to an end;

Dead works that hinder us spiritually.

An Example Of Pruning

Acts 14:27–28 English Standard Version

7 And when they arrived and gathered the church together, they declared all that God had done with them, and how he had opened a door of faith to the Gentiles. 28 And they remained no little time with the disciples.

Acts 15:1–2 Expanded Bible

15 Then some people came to Antioch from Judea and began teaching the Gentile believers [L brothers (and sisters)]: “You cannot be saved if you are not circumcised ·as Moses taught us [L according to the custom of Moses; compare Gen. 17:9–14].” 2 Paul and Barnabas ·were against this teaching and argued [L had no small argument and debate] with them about it. So the church ·decided to send [commissioned; appointed] Paul, Barnabas, and some others to Jerusalem where they could talk more about this disagreement with the apostles and elders.

Paul and Barnabas had completed a mission in which many Gentiles had come to saving faith in Jesus. A sect of Christians from Jerusalem came and told Paul and Barnabas that the converted Gentiles had to follow the Old Testament to be right with God. If ever there was a time when the Father needed to prune the Church, it was now.

As a reminder, at this time, the only Bible Christians had was the Old Testament. Paul and Barnabas preached that Christians did not have to keep the Law to be right with God, which was a radical concept given the long history of Jews following the Law. The opponents to Paul and Barnabas believed that believers needed to trust Jesus and observe Moses’ teaching to be right with God. The churches in Antioch sought counsel from the Christian leaders in Jerusalem to settle this debate.

The apostles in Jerusalem concluded that the Jewish and Gentile believers did not need to keep the law of Moses to be saved. In doing so, the Holy Spirit removed a deadly doctrine that threatened the vitality of the Church.

Resistance To Pruning

We must note that despite the decision of the Apostles and church elders, some in the church could not accept this decision. At first, they started as pious Jewish believers who were misinformed about their faith in Christ. However, they couldn’t receive correction. Their pride caused them to change into enemies of the faith, and they worked against the Gospel, hounding Paul and others, seeking to subvert the work of missions to the Gentiles.

Even today, one of the persistent heresies in the church are false teachers who try to convince believers that they must observe Old Testament practices to be right with God. Often, these are well-meaning, intelligent believers who cannot accept correction. Instead of receiving the discipline of the word, their pride transforms them into enemies of grace.

The New Testament epistles are replete with examples of individuals who resist the Father’s correction from the Word of God. Unlike rose bushes and pear trees, humans can resist pruning! We can protect ourselves against becoming an enemy of grace by being instantly responsive to God’s correction in the following ways:

Read the Bible regularly. When you read a passage that exposes sin or wrongdoing, repent and ask the Holy Spirit to help you overcome that sin.

Be quick to admit you are wrong, misinformed, or in error. There is no shame in that. Being in error is part of being human. Jesus gave us the Holy Spirit to lead us from error into truth (John 14:16–17).

Remember that the Bible cuts! It is sharper than a two-edged sword (Heb. 4:12). Therefore, while temporarily unpleasant, the piercing, cutting, and jabbing of the Scripture is for our good. Don’t resist it, but rather give thanks for it.

Application of Pruning at the Church Level

2 Corinthians 8:7–13 New Living Translation

7 Since you excel in so many ways—in your faith, your gifted speakers, your knowledge, your enthusiasm, and your love from us—I want you to excel also in this gracious act of giving.

8 I am not commanding you to do this. But I am testing how genuine your love is by comparing it with the eagerness of the other churches.

9 You know the generous grace of our Lord Jesus Christ. Though he was rich, yet for your sakes he became poor, so that by his poverty he could make you rich.

10 Here is my advice: It would be good for you to finish what you started a year ago. Last year you were the first who wanted to give, and you were the first to begin doing it. 11 Now you should finish what you started. Let the eagerness you showed in the beginning be matched now by your giving. Give in proportion to what you have. 12 Whatever you give is acceptable if you give it eagerly. And give according to what you have, not what you don’t have. 13 Of course, I don’t mean your giving should make life easy for others and hard for yourselves. I only mean that there should be some equality.

If you face a financial challenge, allow the Word of God to speak to and prune you to meet it adequately. We serve the most high God, who is the creator of heaven and earth. There is no god above Him! All riches, honor, and power belong to him, and he has given all authority in heaven and on earth to Jesus Christ. Jesus has been given a name greater than every other name, not only in this age but in every age.

The Lord Jesus is the mediator of a better covenant based on better promises. As new creatures in Christ Jesus, we are not living under the Old Covenant but in the New Covenant through Christ. We are not sacrificing animals, giving grain offerings, or observing feast days to be right with God. Our right standing with God comes exclusively through faith in the finished work of Jesus Christ. Because Jesus finished his work and obtained for us eternal redemption, there is no need for us to bargain with God. We are his children; we come before his throne in the name of children, boldly make our requests known, and receive the answers to our petitions by faith in his Word.

No More Gimmicks

As believers, we do not need any gimmicks, promotions, or clever tactics to get us to give. All we need to do is to be obedient to our covenant. God has clearly shown us in 2 Corinthians 8,9 what we are required to do as a church body:

To give with the right attitude, that is, eagerly, sacrificially, and with love in our hearts,

To give according to what we have, not according to what we do not have, and

To not give under pressure.

Pruning #1

Some of us have slowed considerably in giving because we do not see progress. We think, “I am tired of putting money into my local church. Nothing ever seems to get done. I don’t want to put good money after bad.” Here is how the word will prune that attitude:

Ultimately, your money is not yours. We are called to give a portion of it to God’s work (Provers 3:9–10)

If God has planted you at a church, you are called to support this ministry financially (1 Cor. 9:1–12).

Pruning #2

Some of you have slowed or stopped giving because you are looking at circumstances. You are either looking at circumstances in your life or the life of your church. You are looking hard at your retirement accounts and financial obligations. Perhaps your local church badly needs repair, and you are discouraged by how it looks.  Your attitude is, “I don’t have enough to sow. I only have enough money to live day by day.”

Here is the correcting word:

In 1 Kings 17, God commanded a widow at Zarephath to feed Elijah during a famine. Even though God spoke to her, she was reluctant to follow through because there was no food and water in the land, and she only had enough food to feed herself and her son for one more day. Her obedience in trusting God by giving to the man of God first made all the difference in her family's survival.

Note that God commanded the widow to give to the man of God first. In other words, Elijah would benefit before she and her family would benefit. That was a hard choice, but if the widow never gave to Elijah, she would have died. But because she was obedient, she not only lived, she prospered. That is how God works.

Pruning #3

Some of you are just being sluggish and listless in your giving. Unless your pastor or a deacon rides you hard, you won’t give to the church. Here is the correcting word:

It is time to grow up as a believer. You don’t need a pledge card, a matching drive, or any special event or announcement to give. That is for babies. You are a mature believer who has walked with the Lord for years. You have the Bible. You should know what to do.

I’m not saying it's wrong for church leaders to send letters or remind believers of their commitments. I am saying that believers must realize that giving to their local church is their responsibility (and privilege).

After we get paid, the first check we should write should be for the Lord. What is so hard about that?

Conclusion

Although I used our attitudes and behavior regarding giving financial gifts to the church, the Lord uses His Word to speak to all areas of our lives. Let’s be diligent about keeping the Scriptures a daily part of our lives and sensitive to the Holy Spirit's voice when he corrects us with the Word of God.

In the name of God, Amen.
